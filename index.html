History of the World Cup

The world cup is an international association football competition contested by the senior men's national teams of the members of Fédération Internationale de Football Association (FIFA). The 20 World Cup tournaments have been won by eight different national teams. Brazil have won five times, and they are the only team to have played in every tournament. The other World Cup winners are Germany and Italy, with four titles each; Argentina and inaugural winners Uruguay, with two titles each; and England, France and Spain, with one title each.

The Golden Ball winner is awarded to the best player at the tournament
The Golden Boot winner is awarded to the player with the most goals at the tournament
The Golden Glove winner is awarded to the goaltender with the most clean sheets
